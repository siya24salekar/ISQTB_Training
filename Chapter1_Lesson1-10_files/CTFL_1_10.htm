Principle 4 – Defect Clustering

When a software development is complete, its good practice to analyze not only the quantity of defects found during the development, but also where the defects were found.

 

For example, using our application, we may have found most issues around the input fields. This we can say is a defect cluster. The defect clusters help us identify areas that were most problematic from a quality perspective. With this information may decide to focus the testing more in this area in future iterations of the product. If the defect clusters are identified early (i.e. before the final testing phases), we may concentrate in this area more heavily during regression testing or acceptance testing.

Make sure you understand the following keywords associated with this lesson: 

Acceptance testing:

Formal testing with respect to user needs, requirements, and business processes conducted to determine whether or not a system satisfies the acceptance criteria and to enable the user, customers or other authorized entity to determine whether or not to accept the system.

Defect:

A flaw in a component or system that can cause the component or system to fail to perform its required function, e.g. an incorrect statement or data definition. A defect, if encountered during execution, may cause a failure of the component or system.
