What can cause a defect?

Before we cover the causes of a defect, there are important distinctions to be made with regards to the terminology related to defects. We can learn these by following a simple scenario:

1. A developer who is writing some software makes a mistake (or error). The terms ‘error’ and ‘mistake’ can be used interchangeably as they are exactly the same.

2. This mistake can produce a ‘defect’. The defect does not necessarily have to belong to actual software code, it can also be in a document or in a system. In addition to the term ‘defect’, you will also come across the terms ‘fault’ and ‘bug’. The term ‘bug’ is probably the most common out of the three, but is gradually being superseded by the term ‘defect’. This is thought to be because the term ‘bug’ can be seen as negative and implies the software developer is at immediate fault.

3. Now at this stage nothing has gone wrong with the software/system as the code has not been executed (i.e. run). So now let’s imagine the code is executed; now what will happen is the defect will do something it shouldn’t, which we can term as a ‘failure’. Not all defects cause failures though, so we cannot assume that all defects will result in the software behaving unexpectedly.

So why does a defect occur?

There is no simple answer to this question as it really depends on the specifics of the software / system etc. and its surrounding environment. For example, a defect may occur because the developer may have made a simple mistake when coding the software. We could analyze why they made the mistake. It may be due to time pressure to get the work complete for a tight deadline, or maybe the sheer complexity of the software, system or associated technologies. Humans do make mistakes; it’s only natural as we are fallible. Not all defects are caused by human error though; some defects can be down to certain environmental conditions such as radiation, magnetism, electronic fields and even pollution. These conditions can affect the software by changing the hardware conditions.

Make sure you understand the following keywords associated with this lesson: 

Bug: Same meaning as 'Defect'

Defect: A flaw in a component or system that can cause the component or system to fail to perform its required function, e.g. an incorrect statement or data definition. A defect, if encountered during execution, may cause a failure of the component or system.

Error: A human action that produces an incorrect result.

Mistake: Same meaning as 'Error'
