Lesson 5-5

Test Estimation

When considering test estimation, we are simply aiming to predict how long certain testing activities will take to complete. This information is extremely useful for project managers to use in their planning. The two most common methods for test estimation are:

The metrics-based approach
This method estimates the testing effort by looking at previous metrics of similar projects, and subsequently calculating the testing effort from them. Careful consideration should be made when using this method to ensure that all aspects of the projects are compared to ensure the accuracy of the estimation.

The expert-based approach:
This estimation approach works by using the estimates provided by the owners of the individual tasks or by experts in the related fields.

When the test effort has been estimated, testing resources can be selected and subsequent schedules can be created. Additional factors which can effect the testing effort include:

  • Characteristics of the product

  • The test basis

  • Size of the product

  • Complexity of the problem domain

  • Requirements for reliability, security, and documentation

  • Characteristics of the development process

  • Stability of the organization

  • Tools used

  • Test process

  • Skills of the people involved

  • Time pressure

  • Outcome of the testing

  • Number of defects

  • Amount of rework needed
