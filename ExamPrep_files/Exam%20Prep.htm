Exam Preparation Tips

One week before the exam

OK, so you have now booked your exam. The week leading up to the exam should be spent reading through the course material again. But this time after each chapter, stop and ask yourself questions about what you have just read. This way you can ensure you are taking in the information and understand it fully

Take the practice quizzes included in the course. If you get a question wrong, review the question and determine why you got the answer wrong. You can always read the associated section again in the course.

If there is something you are not sure about, don't forget if you have signed up for the 'Silver' training package, you can ask your course tutor.

Its natural to feel anxiety before any exam. So try and look at the big picture. You can always re-take the exam if it doesn't go to plan, its not the end of the world. By repeatedly doing the practice quizzes, you can build your confidence levels prior to taking the exam.

 

One day before the exam

Try to ensure you have a relaxing evening planned the night before your exam. Some people like to study the night before, but this different for each individual. You may be better off relaxing and simply doing something you enjoy instead. By now, you have done the studying, so its just a matter of getting in the right frame of mind for the exam.

Make sure you know how to get to the exam centre. Maybe print out a route and have it ready to take with you. If you are taking a bus or train to get there, make sure you have double-checked the times. You don't want to have to rush anything on the day of your exam. Se if there is a place nearby to the exam centre to get a drink and relax before the exam.

 

Exam day

Its here at last, the day you have been waiting for. This is your way to demonstrate your knowledge of software testing. The hard work has been done, you have done all the studying you need to do, and now you can prove to yourself how much you know.....its going to be fun!

Plan to leave for your exam with plenty of time to spare, the last thing you want to have to do is rush out of the house and panic about getting there on time.

Make sure you take a drink with you. Most exam centre's allow drinks to be taken into the exam, check with them first when you book the exam. A drink is really useful during the exam, as it makes you stop thinking for a few seconds and acts like a short break which can help you immensely.

The exam will be multiple-choice. It is very important you answer every question there, you won't get any marks for not answering. You should be able to eliminate at least one or two incorrect answers, which then leaves you with a simpler decision to make for your attempt at the right answer.

In order to pass the exam you need to score 26 correct answers out of a possible 40. This needs to be achieved in one hour. The questions are not long or complex, so you should have plenty of time to spend on each question.

 

We wish you the best of luck with your exam!
